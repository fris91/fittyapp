UX strategy — the short version

Fitty should feel like a calm, encouraging companion — not a dashboard you have to operate. Bright, soft, and personal. Every screen answers one question: “what’s one good thing I can do right now?”

1 · Vision

A pocket wellness companion that turns scattered health data into one gentle next step a day. Fitty unifies body metrics, workouts, nutrition and connected devices, then uses a lightweight AI coach to translate it all into plain, motivating guidance. Premium and friendly — never clinical, never a chore.

2 · Design principles

  • One screen, one job. Progressive disclosure over density. Detail lives a tap deeper, never up front.
  • Encourage, don’t grade. Soft language, celebrate streaks, never shame a missed day.
  • Fast in, fast out. Logging anything is ≤2 taps via the center action button and bottom sheets.
  • Wellness ≠ medical. Guidance is clearly framed as wellness; clinical-looking numbers always carry a quiet disclaimer and a “this isn’t medical advice” boundary.
  • Trustworthy AI. Every AI plan is editable, explainable (“why this?”), and shows what data it used.
  • Soft & physical. Rounded cards, pastel signals, gentle motion. Color carries meaning: coral = action, mint = health/positive, lavender = AI/insight, amber = attention.

3 · Mobile information architecture

Five-tab bottom nav with a raised center “+” for instant logging. Everything else is reachable in ≤2 levels.

🏠 Today📈 Progress＋ Log🥗 Plans✨ Coach
  • Today — home/dashboard, daily focus, check-in, recommendations.
  • Progress — weight, body metrics, history charts, goals, health score.
  • Log (＋) — bottom sheet: meal · workout · body data · water · mood.
  • Plans — workout & nutrition plans, recipes, AI generation entry points.
  • Coach — AI chat, recommendations feed, weekly review.
  • Profile/Settings — avatar in header → profile, integrations, subscription, privacy, notifications.

4 · The daily engagement loop

You said the daily habit is simply “be healthy.” That’s broad, so the loop is built around three tiny touchpoints that add up — designed so even a 15-second visit feels complete:

  • Morning check-in — a single tap on mood/energy + today’s one focus card.
  • Midday log — one meal or workout via the ＋ button; instant positive feedback + ring fills.
  • Evening reflection — Coach summarizes the day in one friendly line and previews tomorrow.

↳ React to this loop — if “be healthy” really means weight loss, muscle gain, or sleep, I’ll re-center the whole home screen around that goal.

5 · Three home concepts — which to build

The home screen is the daily battleground. Three honest directions, compared:

A · Data-focused
The cockpit

Layout: metric grid up top (rings + numbers), trend strip, recent logs.

Strengths: power users feel in control; everything visible.

Weak: can feel clinical / overwhelming; against “not a dashboard” goal.

Best for: quantified-self, athletes, device-heavy users.

B · AI-coach
The companion

Layout: conversational hero — coach greeting + today’s one move, then a feed of suggestions.

Strengths: warmest, lowest cognitive load, most “alive”.

Weak: data feels buried; needs strong AI to earn trust.

Best for: beginners, guidance-seekers, daily re-engagement.

★ C · Habit / progress
The momentum board

Layout: big “today” focus + streak/rings, then this-week momentum, with the coach as a friendly inline voice.

Strengths: drives daily return, motivating, balances data + warmth.

Weak: needs real streak logic to not feel gimmicky.

Best for: the broad “be healthy” mainstream user — your target.

Recommendation: Ship Concept C (Habit/Progress) as the default home, borrowing B’s coach voice as an inline card and exposing A’s full metric grid one tap away under Progress. It best serves a mainstream “be healthy” audience while leaving room for power users. See all three live in the Home concepts tab.
